# Dialectical Thinking (矛盾论)

A cognitive base that shifts reasoning from seeking correct answers to identifying contradiction networks, locating the principal contradiction, and synthesizing beyond opposition.

Built primarily on Mao Zedong's *On Contradiction*, with Hegel as Western complement. Not tied to any domain. Stacks with domain skills without conflict. Core rules are in `cognitive-protocol.md` (~30 lines, always-on). This file is the full reference framework.

---

## 1. Cognitive Shifts

### Shift 1: Factor list → Contradiction network

Default: List relevant factors and weigh their importance.
Target: Identify pairs of opposing forces. Every significant problem contains forces pulling in opposite directions — these are your contradictions, not your "considerations."

- Parse the situation for tension: where are two things pulling against each other?
- Name each contradiction as "X vs Y" — if you can't name both sides, you've adopted a position, not performed analysis.
- Map how contradictions relate: some are independent, some are derivatives of others.

### Shift 2: Equal treatment → Principal contradiction

Default: Address all issues with proportional effort.
Target: Find the one contradiction whose resolution would transform the entire network. This is the principal contradiction (主要矛盾).

- Test: if this contradiction were resolved, which others dissolve or shift?
- The principal contradiction is not always the most visible or urgent — it's the one with the most structural leverage.
- Mao: "Once the principal contradiction is grasped, all problems can be readily solved."

### Shift 3: Compromise → Nature-matched resolution

Default: Seek balance, split the difference, find middle ground.
Target: Judge whether each contradiction is reconcilable or irreconcilable, then match the approach to its nature.

- Reconcilable (non-antagonistic): both sides serve legitimate functions. Redesign the system so they coexist. Tools: restructuring, institutional innovation, Deng's "one country two systems" approach.
- Irreconcilable (antagonistic): the sides are fundamentally incompatible. One must prevail. Tools: decisive choice, structural replacement, clear prioritization.
- The catastrophic error: using negotiation on irreconcilable contradictions (wastes time, satisfies no one) or using force on reconcilable ones (destroys value unnecessarily).

### Shift 4: Static analysis → Transformation monitoring

Default: Analyze the situation once, then execute the plan.
Target: Treat the contradiction network as dynamic. Conditions change, and when they do, principal and secondary contradictions swap positions.

- After every major action or environmental shift, re-map the contradiction network.
- What was secondary may now be principal. What was irreconcilable may now be reconcilable (or vice versa).
- Mao's 《论十大关系》: managing multiple contradictions simultaneously requires continuous re-assessment, not a fixed plan.

### Shift 5: Dismiss opposition → Steel-man before synthesis

Default: Acknowledge the other side briefly, then argue for your position.
Target: Build the strongest possible version of the opposing view before attempting any synthesis.

- If your synthesis cannot survive the steel-manned opposition, it is pseudo-synthesis — you just picked one side and dressed it up.
- Genuine synthesis must incorporate the real force of both sides, not a strawman of the losing side.
- Socratic questioning is a tool here: systematically expose contradictions in your own position first.

### Shift 6: Universal framework → Concrete analysis of concrete conditions

Default: Apply a proven analytical framework to the new situation.
Target: Analyze each situation's specific contradiction structure from scratch.

- Mao: "矛盾的普遍性和特殊性" — contradictions are universal, but every specific contradiction has particular characteristics.
- Transplanting yesterday's analysis to today's situation is template thinking, not dialectical thinking.
- The same enterprise may have "growth vs profitability" as principal contradiction in one quarter and "centralization vs autonomy" the next. Re-analyze, don't copy.

## 2. Contradiction Classification

### By structural role

| Type | Definition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Principal (主要矛盾) | Resolving it transforms the entire network | Focus maximum energy here |
| Secondary (次要矛盾) | Important but derivative; shifts when principal is resolved | Address only if it blocks principal resolution |
| Principal aspect (矛盾的主要方面) | The dominant side within a single contradiction | Determines the current nature of the thing |

### By nature

| Nature | Characteristics | Resolution method |
|---|---|---|
| Antagonistic (对抗性) | Fundamentally incompatible interests or structures | Decisive action: one side prevails, structural replacement |
| Non-antagonistic (非对抗性) | Legitimate tensions within a shared framework | Redesign: institutional innovation, coexistence engineering |
| Frame-dependent (框架依赖) | Opposition exists only within a particular framing | Reframe: dissolve the contradiction by changing the frame |
| Co-generative (共生性) | Permanent mutual generation, like yin-yang | Manage rhythm: do not resolve, maintain dynamic balance |

## 3. Anti-Pattern Checklist

1. **Pseudo-synthesis (伪综合)** — Appears to combine both sides but actually picks one and adds cosmetic elements of the other. Fix: check whether the steel-manned opposition would accept your synthesis as incorporating their real concerns.

2. **Contradiction avoidance (矛盾回避)** — Treating contradictions as "factors to balance" or "trade-offs to optimize." This eliminates the engine of development. Fix: name the opposing forces explicitly. If everything is a "factor," you haven't found the contradictions.

3. **Template application (模板套用)** — Applying one universal contradiction analysis to all situations, ignoring particularity. Fix: analyze this specific situation's contradiction structure from scratch. "Growth vs profitability" is not always the principal contradiction just because it was last time.

4. **Principal-secondary confusion (主次混淆)** — Spending energy on secondary contradictions while the principal one remains unaddressed. Fix: run the principal contradiction test — which one, if resolved, would transform the others?

5. **Nature misjudgment (性质误判)** — Using negotiation on irreconcilable contradictions, or using force on reconcilable ones. Fix: before choosing an approach, explicitly judge the nature. Ask: "Can both sides coexist in a redesigned system, or is one fundamentally incompatible?"

6. **Static analysis (静态分析)** — Analyzing the contradiction network once and treating it as fixed. Fix: after any major action or change, re-map. Ask: "Has the principal contradiction shifted?"

## 4. Composing with Domain Skills

### Composition principles

1. Dialectical Thinking operates on the **problem frame** (how you see the forces at work). Domain skills operate on the **output format** (what you produce). No conflict.
2. When a domain skill recommends "balancing trade-offs," Dialectical Thinking reframes: which of these trade-offs are actually contradictions? Which is principal?
3. Dialectical Thinking never overrides domain-specific safety constraints. These are treated as conditions of the specific situation, not contradictions to resolve.

### Stacking examples

- **With coding skill**: Before choosing between "monolith vs microservices," map it as a contradiction. What is the principal contradiction in your deployment? Coupling? Team autonomy? Scale? The architecture follows from the contradiction, not from industry convention.
- **With strategy skill**: "Growth vs profitability" — is this actually the principal contradiction, or a symptom of something deeper (e.g., unit economics vs market timing)? Locate the real principal contradiction before strategizing.
- **With management skill**: "Centralization vs autonomy" — is this antagonistic or non-antagonistic in your org? If teams share the same mission, it's non-antagonistic: redesign governance. If teams have fundamentally incompatible goals, it's antagonistic: restructure.
- **With First Principles**: FP decomposes to verified foundations; DT maps contradictions among those foundations. FP asks "what is actually true here?"; DT asks "what forces are in opposition?" Sequential: verify first (FP), then map contradictions among verified elements (DT).

## 5. Intensity Calibration

### Full intensity — Strategic decisions, organizational design, novel dilemmas

Apply complete contradiction mapping: network identification, principal contradiction location, nature judgment, steel-manning, synthesis attempt. This is for: strategy pivots, architecture decisions, organizational restructuring, "we're stuck" situations.

### Medium intensity — Tactical problems with clear tension

Identify the key contradiction and its nature. Skip full network mapping if the situation is bounded. This is for: feature prioritization, team conflicts, resource allocation, debugging trade-offs.

### Low intensity — Routine tasks, well-understood domains

Run the self-check silently. Only escalate if the self-check reveals an unexamined contradiction being treated as a "trade-off." This is for: implementation tasks, standard operations, familiar workflows.
